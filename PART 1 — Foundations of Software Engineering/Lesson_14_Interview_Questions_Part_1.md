# Section 1 — Basic Interview Questions

## Q1. What is Software Engineering?

**Answer:**

Software Engineering is the systematic application of engineering principles, methods, and tools to the design, development, testing, deployment, and maintenance of software.

---

## Q2. Why was Software Engineering invented?

**Answer:**

Software Engineering was introduced to solve the **Software Crisis**, where projects were failing because of poor planning, increasing complexity, lack of documentation, and inadequate testing.

---

## Q3. What is the Software Crisis?

**Answer:**

The Software Crisis refers to the period during the 1960s–1970s when software projects frequently exceeded budgets, missed deadlines, contained many defects, and were difficult to maintain.

---

## Q4. What is the difference between a Program and Software?

| Program | Software |
|---------|----------|
| Set of instructions | Complete software product |
| Solves one task | Solves business problems |
| Mostly source code | Code + Docs + Config + UI + Database |

---

## Q5. What is SDLC?

**Answer:**

SDLC (Software Development Life Cycle) is a structured process used to build software through phases such as requirements, design, development, testing, deployment, and maintenance.

---

## Q6. List the phases of SDLC.

```text
Requirements
     ↓
Design
     ↓
Development
     ↓
Testing
     ↓
Deployment
     ↓
Maintenance
```

---

## Q7. Why is documentation important?

**Answer:**

Documentation:
- Improves communication
- Simplifies maintenance
- Helps onboarding
- Preserves knowledge
- Supports testing and deployment

# Section 2 — Intermediate Questions

## Q11. Why is modularity important?

**Answer:**

Modularity divides large systems into independent components, making software easier to understand, maintain, test, and extend.

---

## Q12. Differentiate Cohesion and Coupling.

| Cohesion | Coupling |
|----------|----------|
| Within a module | Between modules |
| Prefer High | Prefer Low |

---

## Q13. Why is maintainability important?

Because software changes frequently after release.

Maintainable software reduces:
- Cost
- Time
- Bugs

---

## Q14. Explain abstraction with an example.

Driving a car.

The driver uses the steering wheel without knowing how the engine works.

Software hides implementation details in the same way.

---

## Q15. Explain encapsulation.

Encapsulation keeps data and operations together while hiding internal implementation.

---

# Section 3 — Stakeholders & Roles

## Q16. Who are stakeholders?

People or organizations that influence or are affected by the software project.

Examples:
- Customer
- Product Owner
- Developer
- QA
- DevOps
- End User

---

## Q17. Difference between Product Owner and Project Manager.

| Product Owner | Project Manager |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Product vision | Project execution |
| Prioritizes features | Manages schedule and resources |

---

## Q18. Role of Business Analyst.

- Gather requirements
- Communicate with stakeholders
- Prepare SRS
- Bridge business and technical teams

---

## Q19. Role of Software Architect.

Designs:
- Architecture
- Technology stack
- Scalability
- Security
- High-level design

---

## Q20. Role of QA Engineer.

Ensures software quality through:
- Functional testing
- Regression testing
- Automation
- Performance testing

# Section 4 — Scenario-Based Questions

## Scenario 1

**The client changes requirements halfway through development. What should the team do?**

Expected points:
- Analyze impact
- Update requirements
- Re-estimate effort
- Communicate with stakeholders
- Implement through change management

---

## Scenario 2

**A project is behind schedule. Should the manager simply add more developers?**

Expected answer:

Not necessarily.

According to **Brooks' Law**, adding developers to a late project can increase communication overhead and delay delivery further.

---

## Scenario 3

**Your application works but crashes under heavy traffic. Which quality attribute is weak?**

Answer:

- Scalability
- Reliability
- Performance

---

## Scenario 4

**The application is difficult to modify after deployment. Which quality attribute is poor?**

Answer:

Maintainability.

# Tips for Interviews

- Explain concepts with real-world examples.
- Draw simple SDLC diagrams when possible.
- Differentiate similar terms clearly.
- Mention trade-offs instead of memorized definitions.
- Use structured answers:
  - Definition
  - Why needed
  - Example
  - Benefits
