# Part 1 — Foundations of Software Engineering

> **Objective**
>

---

# What is a Stakeholder?

A **stakeholder** is any individual, group, or organization that can influence a software project or is affected by its outcome.

Simply put:

> If someone builds it, funds it, manages it, uses it, supports it, or is impacted by it, they are a stakeholder.

---

# Why are Stakeholders Important?

Even the best-written software can fail if stakeholders are not aligned.

Example:

```text
Customer Wants A
        |
Business Explains B
        |
Developers Build C
        |
Users Receive D
```

Poor communication leads to project failure.

---

# Internal Stakeholders

People directly involved in building the software.

## 1. Product Owner / Client Representative

Responsibilities:

- Defines business goals
- Prioritizes features
- Maintains product backlog
- Accepts completed work

Acts as the voice of the customer.

---

## 2. Project Manager

Responsibilities:

- Planning
- Budget
- Schedule
- Risk management
- Team coordination

Focus:

```
Time
Cost
Quality
Scope
```

---

## 3. Software Architect

Designs the overall system.

Decides:

- Architecture
- Technology stack
- Scalability
- Security approach
- High-level design

Analogy:

An architect designing a building before construction starts.

---

## 4. Business Analyst (BA)

Acts as the bridge between customers and developers.

Responsibilities:

- Gather requirements
- Clarify business needs
- Create requirement documents
- Validate requirements

```
Customer
    |
Business Analyst
    |
Development Team
```

---

## 5. Software Developers

Convert designs into working software.

Responsibilities:

- Coding
- Unit testing
- Bug fixing
- Code reviews
- Feature implementation

---

## 6. UI/UX Designer

Creates user experience.

Focuses on:

- Interface
- Navigation
- Accessibility
- User satisfaction

---

## 7. QA / Test Engineers

Verify software quality.

Activities:

- Functional testing
- Regression testing
- Performance testing
- Security testing
- Automation testing

Goal:

Deliver reliable software.

---

## 8. DevOps Engineers

Manage deployment and operational processes.

Responsibilities:

- CI/CD pipelines
- Infrastructure
- Monitoring
- Release automation

They help software move safely from development to production.

---

# External Stakeholders

## 9. Customers

Provide funding or request the software.

They define business objectives and approve deliverables.

---

## 10. End Users

People who actually use the software.

Examples:

- Students
- Doctors
- Bank customers
- Employees
- Shoppers

Their feedback drives future improvements.

---

## 11. Investors / Sponsors

Provide financial support.

Interested in:

- Budget
- Profit
- Delivery timeline
- Business value

---

## 12. Government & Regulatory Bodies

Ensure compliance.

Examples:

- Banking regulations
- Healthcare privacy
- Data protection
- Tax regulations

Failure to comply may result in legal consequences.

# Why Stakeholder Communication Matters

Poor communication causes:

- Wrong requirements
- Missed deadlines
- Budget overruns
- Low-quality software
- Customer dissatisfaction

Good communication results in:

- Shared understanding
- Faster decisions
- Better quality
- Fewer defects

---

# Common Misconceptions

❌ Developers alone build successful software.

✔ Successful software requires collaboration across many roles.

❌ Customers disappear after requirements.

✔ Customers and users provide continuous feedback throughout the product lifecycle.

❌ QA only finds bugs.

✔ QA contributes to improving overall software quality.

# Summary

A software project succeeds through the collaboration of many stakeholders, each contributing unique expertise. Customers define business needs, analysts translate requirements, architects design solutions, developers build features, QA engineers verify quality, DevOps engineers deploy software, and end users provide feedback for continuous improvement. Clear communication among stakeholders is essential throughout the software development lifecycle.
