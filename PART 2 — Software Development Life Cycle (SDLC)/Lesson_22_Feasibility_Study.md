# Introduction

Imagine a company wants to build a food delivery application in one month with a budget of ₹2 lakh while expecting to compete with global platforms.

Can it be done?

Maybe.

Should it be done?

That depends.

Before investing time, money, and people, organizations perform a **Feasibility Study**.

---

# What is a Feasibility Study?

A **Feasibility Study** is the process of evaluating whether a proposed software project is practical, achievable, and worth investing in.

It answers three important questions:

```text
Can we build it?
        |
Should we build it?
        |
Is it worth building?
```

---

# Why is a Feasibility Study Needed?

Without feasibility analysis:

```text
Idea
 |
Start Development
 |
Budget Exhausted
 |
Deadlines Missed
 |
Project Cancelled
```

With feasibility analysis:

```text
Idea
 |
Feasibility Study
 |
Approved
 |
SDLC Continues
```

It prevents expensive failures.

# Types of Feasibility

## 1. Technical Feasibility

### Question

Can the technology support the project?

Evaluate:

- Programming languages
- Frameworks
- Cloud platform
- Infrastructure
- Team expertise

Example:

A company wants an AI chatbot.

Questions:

- Do we have AI engineers?
- Can our servers handle AI models?
- Do we need GPUs?

---

## 2. Economic Feasibility

### Question

Is the project financially worthwhile?

Compare:

```text
Project Cost
      |
Expected Benefits
```

Includes:

- Development cost
- Infrastructure
- Salaries
- Licensing
- Maintenance

If benefits outweigh costs, the project may proceed.

---

## 3. Operational Feasibility

### Question

Will users actually use the software?

Evaluate:

- Business processes
- Employee acceptance
- Training needs
- User experience

Example:

If hospital staff refuse to use the new system, technically successful software still fails.

---

## 4. Schedule Feasibility

### Question

Can the project be completed within the required timeline?

Example:

```text
Estimated Time = 12 Months

Required Time = 3 Months

Not Feasible
```

---

## 5. Legal Feasibility

### Question

Does the project comply with laws and regulations?

Examples:

- Data privacy
- Copyright
- Financial regulations
- Healthcare compliance

Ignoring legal requirements can lead to penalties.

---

## 6. Organizational Feasibility

### Question

Does the project align with business goals?

Examples:

- Company strategy
- Available resources
- Management support

---

# TELOS Framework

A popular way to remember feasibility types:

```text
T → Technical
E → Economic
L → Legal
O → Operational
S → Schedule
```

---

# Cost-Benefit Analysis

Organizations estimate:

```text
Total Cost
      |
Compare
      |
Expected Revenue / Savings
```

If benefits exceed costs over time, the project is more attractive.

---

# ROI (Return on Investment)

Simple idea:

```text
Higher Benefit
      -
Higher Cost
      =
ROI
```

Projects with better ROI are usually prioritized.

# Industry Example

## Hospital Management System

### Technical

Can the existing servers handle thousands of patients?

### Economic

Is the investment justified?

### Operational

Will doctors and nurses adopt the system?

### Legal

Does it comply with healthcare regulations?

### Schedule

Can it be deployed before the hospital opens?

Only if these questions are answered positively should development continue.

# Benefits

- Reduces project risk
- Saves money

- Improves planning

---

# Common Mistakes

❌ Ignoring technical limitations.

✔ Evaluate skills and technology first.

❌ Underestimating costs.

✔ Include development, infrastructure, maintenance, and training.

❌ Ignoring user adoption.

✔ Study operational feasibility.

# Summary

A Feasibility Study helps organizations determine whether a software project is practical, affordable, legally compliant, and aligned with business objectives before major investments begin. By evaluating technical, economic, operational, legal, and schedule factors, teams reduce risk and make informed Go/No-Go decisions, laying a strong foundation for successful software development.
