# Lesson 46 — Traditional SDLC Models Interview Questions

# Part 3 — Traditional SDLC Models

> **Objective**
>
> Master interview questions related to traditional SDLC models with concise answers, comparisons, and scenario-based discussions.

---

# Section 1 — Fundamentals

## Q1. What is an SDLC model?

**Answer**

An SDLC model is a structured approach that defines **how the phases of the Software Development Life Cycle are organized and executed** to develop software.

---

## Q2. Why do we need different SDLC models?

Different projects have different:

- Requirement stability
- Risk levels
- Budgets
- Timelines
- Compliance needs
- Team sizes

No single model fits every project.

---

## Q3. Name the major traditional SDLC models.

- Waterfall
- V-Model
- Incremental
- Iterative
- Spiral
- RAD
- Prototype
- Big Bang
- Component-Based Development (CBD)

---

# Section 2 — Waterfall Model

## Q4. What is the Waterfall Model?

A sequential SDLC model where each phase is completed before the next begins.

### Best For

- Stable requirements
- Compliance-heavy projects

### Limitation

Requirement changes are expensive.

---

## Q5. Give one real-world use case for Waterfall.

Government systems, banking core systems, and defense software.

---

# Section 3 — V-Model

## Q6. What is the V-Model?

An extension of Waterfall where every development phase has a corresponding testing phase.

---

## Q7. Differentiate Verification and Validation.

| Verification | Validation |
|--------------|------------|
| Are we building the product correctly? | Are we building the right product? |
| Reviews | Software execution |

---

# Section 4 — Incremental & Iterative

## Q8. What is the Incremental Model?

Software is delivered in multiple functional releases.

---

## Q9. What is the Iterative Model?

Software is improved repeatedly through multiple iterations.

---

## Q10. Incremental vs Iterative

| Incremental | Iterative |
|-------------|-----------|
| Adds new features | Improves existing features |
| Focus on expansion | Focus on refinement |

---

# Section 5 — Spiral

## Q11. Why is the Spiral Model called risk-driven?

Because every development cycle includes formal risk analysis before implementation.

---

## Q12. Name the four quadrants of the Spiral Model.

1. Planning
2. Risk Analysis
3. Engineering
4. Customer Evaluation

---

# Section 6 — RAD & Prototype

## Q13. What is RAD?

A model focused on rapid development using prototyping, reusable components, and continuous customer involvement.

---

## Q14. Why is the Prototype Model used?

To clarify unclear or evolving requirements before full-scale development.

---

# Section 7 — Big Bang & CBD

## Q15. When should the Big Bang Model be used?

For:

- Research
- Experiments
- Proof of Concept (PoC)
- Very small projects

---

## Q16. What is Component-Based Development?

A model where software is built by integrating reusable software components.

---

# Section 8 — Model Selection

## Q17. Which model is best for stable requirements?

Waterfall or V-Model.

---

## Q18. Which model is best for rapidly changing requirements?

Iterative or Prototype.

---

## Q19. Which model is best for high-risk projects?

Spiral.

---

## Q20. Which model is best for fast delivery?

RAD.

---

## Q21. Which model is best for enterprise systems with reusable modules?

Component-Based Development.

---

# Section 9 — Scenario Questions

## Scenario 1

The customer changes requirements every week.

**Answer**

Avoid Waterfall.

Prefer:

- Iterative
- Prototype
- RAD

---

## Scenario 2

You are developing flight-control software.

Which model would you choose?

**Answer**

V-Model due to strong verification, validation, and traceability.

---

## Scenario 3

A startup wants an MVP in two months.

Recommended model?

**Answer**

RAD or Incremental.

---

## Scenario 4

The project has high technical uncertainty and significant financial risk.

Recommended model?

**Answer**

Spiral.

---

## Scenario 5

A university team is testing a brand-new AI algorithm.

Recommended model?

**Answer**

Big Bang or Prototype.

# Interview Tips

- Start with a definition.
- Explain *why* the model exists.
- Mention advantages and limitations.
- State ideal use cases.
- Compare it with at least one other model.
- Support answers with a practical example.

---

# Summary

Interviewers expect more than definitions. They want you to explain **why a model exists, when it should be used, when it should be avoided, and how it compares with other models**. Understanding these trade-offs demonstrates practical software engineering knowledge.
