# 1. Agile Overview

## What is Agile?

Agile is an iterative software development approach that focuses on:

- Frequent delivery
- Customer collaboration
- Continuous improvement
- Responding to change

```text
Plan
 ↓
Build
 ↓

 ↓

 ↓

 ↺
```

---

# 2. Why Agile Was Introduced

Problems with traditional development:

- Slow delivery
- Late customer feedback
- Expensive changes
- Long release cycles

Agile solves this through:

- Small iterations
- Continuous feedback
- Adaptive planning

# 4. Twelve Agile Principles

1. Customer satisfaction through early delivery
2. Welcome changing requirements
3. Deliver frequently
4. Business and developers work together
5. Build around motivated individuals
6. Prefer direct communication
7. Working software is the main progress measure
8. Maintain sustainable development pace
9. Focus on technical excellence
10. Simplicity is important
11. Self-organizing teams
12. Continuous improvement

# 15. Interview Quick Answers

## What is Agile?

An iterative approach that delivers software incrementally while adapting to change.

## Why Agile?

To improve speed, flexibility, collaboration, and customer satisfaction.

## What is Sprint?

A fixed time period where a team builds a working increment.

## What is Velocity?

Amount of work completed in a sprint.

## What is Definition of Done?

Criteria that determine when work is complete.

---

# 16. Agile Selection Guide

Use Agile when:

✓ Requirements change frequently  
✓ Fast releases are needed  
✓ Customer feedback is important  
✓ Product evolves continuously  

Avoid pure Agile when:

- Requirements are completely fixed
- Regulations demand strict sequential approval
