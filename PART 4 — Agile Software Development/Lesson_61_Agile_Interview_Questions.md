# Lesson 61 — Agile Interview Questions

# Part 4 — Agile Software Development

> **Objective**
>
> Prepare for Agile-related interviews by understanding frequently asked questions, scenario-based discussions, and practical explanations used in software engineering interviews.

---

# Section 1 — Agile Fundamentals

## Q1. What is Agile?

**Answer:**

Agile is a software development approach that focuses on iterative development, continuous customer collaboration, frequent delivery, and responding to changing requirements.

---

## Q2. Why was Agile introduced?

Agile was introduced to overcome limitations of traditional models like Waterfall:

- Slow delivery
- Late feedback
- Expensive requirement changes
- Limited customer involvement

---

## Q3. Is Agile a methodology or a mindset?

Agile is primarily a mindset based on values and principles.

Frameworks like Scrum and Kanban implement Agile practices.

---

# Section 2 — Agile Manifesto

## Q4. What are the four Agile values?

1. Individuals and interactions over processes and tools
2. Working software over comprehensive documentation
3. Customer collaboration over contract negotiation
4. Responding to change over following a plan

---

## Q5. Does Agile mean no documentation?

No.

Agile values useful documentation but avoids unnecessary documentation that does not provide value.

---

# Section 3 — Agile Principles

## Q6. How many Agile principles exist?

There are 12 Agile principles.

They focus on:

- Customer satisfaction
- Frequent delivery
- Collaboration
- Technical excellence
- Continuous improvement

---

## Q7. What does "responding to change" mean?

Agile teams adapt to new requirements instead of strictly following an outdated plan.

---

# Section 5 — Agile Roles

## Q9. What is the role of Product Owner?

Responsibilities:

- Define product vision
- Prioritize backlog
- Represent customer needs
- Accept completed work

---

## Q10. What does a Scrum Master do?

Responsibilities:

- Facilitate Scrum events
- Remove blockers
- Coach the team
- Promote Agile practices

---

## Q11. Is Scrum Master a Project Manager?

No.

A Scrum Master is a facilitator and coach, not a traditional manager.

---

# Section 6 — Agile Ceremonies

## Q12. What are Agile ceremonies?

Common Scrum ceremonies:

- Sprint Planning
- Daily Stand-up
- Sprint Review
- Sprint Retrospective
- Backlog Refinement

---

## Q13. What happens in Daily Stand-up?

Team members discuss:

1. What was completed?
2. What will be done next?
3. Any blockers?

---

## Q14. Why is Sprint Retrospective important?

It helps teams identify improvements in:

- Process
- Communication
- Technical practices

---

# Section 7 — Agile Artifacts

## Q15. What are Scrum artifacts?

Three main artifacts:

1. Product Backlog
2. Sprint Backlog
3. Product Increment

---

## Q16. Who owns the Product Backlog?

Product Owner.

---

## Q17. What is Definition of Done?

A shared quality checklist that defines when work is considered complete.

---

# Section 8 — Agile Estimation

## Q18. What are Story Points?

Story Points estimate relative effort, complexity, and uncertainty of a user story.

---

## Q19. Why does Agile use Fibonacci numbers?

Because larger tasks have higher uncertainty.

Example:

```text
1 2 3 5 8 13 21
```

---

## Q20. Explain Planning Poker.

A team estimation technique where members independently estimate effort and discuss differences before reaching consensus.

---

# Section 9 — Agile Metrics

## Q21. What is Velocity?

Velocity is the number of story points completed by a team in a sprint.

---

## Q22. Difference between Lead Time and Cycle Time?

| Lead Time | Cycle Time |
|-----------|------------|
| Request to delivery | Start to completion |
| Customer view | Development view |

---

## Q23. What is a Burndown Chart?

A chart showing remaining work over time during a sprint.

---

# Section 10 — Scenario Questions

## Scenario 1

A customer changes requirements every week.

Which approach is suitable?

**Answer:**

Agile because it supports changing requirements through iterative development.

---

## Scenario 2

A team completes features but quality is poor.

What should they improve?

**Answer:**

Focus on:

- Definition of Done
- Automated testing
- Code reviews
- Technical practices

---

## Scenario 3

Developers are blocked by infrastructure issues.

Who helps remove blockers?

**Answer:**

Scrum Master helps coordinate resolution.

---

## Scenario 4

The Product Owner adds too much work during a sprint.

What should happen?

**Answer:**

Protect the Sprint Goal and discuss changes with the team.

---

# Section 11 — Comparison Questions

## Agile vs Waterfall

| Agile | Waterfall |
|------|-----------|
| Iterative | Sequential |
| Frequent releases | Single release |
| Change friendly | Change expensive |
| Continuous feedback | Feedback at end |

# Interview Tips

- Explain concepts with examples.
- Do not only memorize definitions.
- Connect Agile practices to real projects.
- Mention trade-offs.
- Explain why a practice exists.

---

# Summary

Agile interviews test practical understanding rather than memorized definitions. A strong candidate should explain why Agile exists, how teams work, how Scrum operates, how estimation and metrics help planning, and how Agile principles improve software delivery.
