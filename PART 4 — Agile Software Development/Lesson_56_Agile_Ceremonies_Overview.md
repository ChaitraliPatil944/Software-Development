# Introduction

Agile teams do not simply write code.

They regularly communicate, plan, inspect progress, and improve.

These structured activities are called **Agile Ceremonies**.

```text
Plan
  ↓
Build
  ↓
Review
  ↓
Improve
  ↺
```

---

# What are Agile Ceremonies?

Agile ceremonies are recurring meetings that help teams:

- Plan work
- Coordinate daily progress
- Demonstrate completed work
- Improve the development process

They are most commonly associated with **Scrum**.

# Ceremony 1 — Sprint Planning

## Purpose

Decide:

- What will be built?
- Why is it important?
- How will the team deliver it?

## Participants

- Product Owner
- Scrum Master
- Developers

## Inputs

- Product Backlog
- Business priorities
- Team capacity

## Outputs

- Sprint Goal
- Sprint Backlog

---

# Ceremony 2 — Daily Stand-up

A short daily meeting, usually **15 minutes**.

Each team member answers:

1. What did I complete yesterday?
2. What will I work on today?
3. Is anything blocking me?

```text
Yesterday
    ↓
Today
    ↓
Blockers
```

Purpose:

- Synchronize the team
- Identify blockers early

---

# Ceremony 3 — Sprint Review

Held at the end of the sprint.

Purpose:

- Demonstrate completed work
- Collect stakeholder feedback

Participants:

- Agile Team
- Product Owner
- Stakeholders

Output:

- Accepted work
- New feedback
- Updated Product Backlog

---

# Ceremony 4 — Sprint Retrospective

Purpose:

Improve how the team works.

Common discussion:

```text
What went well?
        ↓
What didn't?
        ↓
How can we improve?
```

Participants:

- Scrum Team only

Output:

- Action items for the next sprint

---

# Ceremony 5 — Backlog Refinement

Sometimes called **Backlog Grooming**.

Purpose:

- Clarify upcoming work
- Split large stories
- Estimate effort
- Re-prioritize items

The Product Backlog stays healthy and ready for future Sprint Planning.

# Industry Example

## Online Shopping Platform

Sprint Goal:

Enable online payments.

Planning:
- Select payment stories.

Daily Stand-ups:
- Track API integration.

Sprint Review:
- Demonstrate successful checkout.

Retrospective:
- Improve testing strategy.

Refinement:
- Prepare refund stories.

# Ceremony Comparison

| Ceremony | Purpose | Output |
|----------|---------|--------|
| Sprint Planning | Plan sprint | Sprint Backlog |
| Daily Stand-up | Coordinate work | Blockers identified |
| Sprint Review | Demo increment | Feedback |
| Sprint Retrospective | Improve process | Action items |
| Backlog Refinement | Prepare future work | Refined backlog |

# Benefits

# Common Mistakes

❌ Turning stand-ups into long problem-solving meetings.

✔ Discuss blockers separately after the stand-up.

❌ Skipping retrospectives.

✔ Always improve the process.

❌ Treating Sprint Reviews as status meetings.

✔ Demonstrate working software.

# Summary

Agile ceremonies provide the rhythm of Scrum-based development. Sprint Planning defines the work, Daily Stand-ups synchronize the team, Sprint Reviews gather feedback, Retrospectives improve the process, and Backlog Refinement prepares future work. Together, these ceremonies ensure continuous communication, transparency, and delivery of customer value.
