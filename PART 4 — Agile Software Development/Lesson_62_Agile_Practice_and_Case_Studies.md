## 2. Explain Sprint Planning.

Include:

- Selecting backlog items

---

## 3. Difference between Product Backlog and Sprint Backlog.

| Product Backlog | Sprint Backlog |
|---|---|
| Entire product work | Current sprint work |
| Owned by Product Owner | Managed by Developers |

---

## 4. Explain Velocity.

Velocity is the amount of work completed by a team in a sprint, usually measured in story points.

# Case Study 1 — E-Commerce Application

## Situation

A company wants to build an online shopping platform.

Requirements:

- Frequent customer feedback
- Weekly feature releases
- Changing priorities

## Questions

1. Why is Agile suitable?
2. Which ceremonies will be used?
3. Which metrics can help track progress?

## Expected Answer

Agile is suitable because requirements change frequently.

Use:

- Sprint Planning
- Daily Stand-up
- Sprint Review
- Retrospective

Metrics:

- Velocity
- Burndown
- Cycle Time

# Scenario-Based Questions

## Scenario 1

A developer says:

"I finished coding, so my task is complete."

Is this correct?

Answer:

No.

Work is complete only when it satisfies the Definition of Done.

---

## Scenario 2

A Product Owner keeps changing sprint requirements.

What should the team do?

Answer:

Protect the Sprint Goal and discuss changes through proper backlog prioritization.

---

## Scenario 3

The team misses sprint goals repeatedly.

Possible improvements:

- Review estimation
- Improve backlog refinement
- Reduce excessive workload
- Analyze blockers

---

## Scenario 4

A team has high velocity but unhappy customers.

What could be wrong?

Possible reasons:

- Wrong priorities
- Low business value features
- Poor customer collaboration

# Summary

Practice converts Agile theory into engineering decision-making ability. By analyzing real-world situations, teams learn when to prioritize features, how to handle changing requirements, how to improve delivery, and how to balance speed with quality.

Agile is not just about running meetings. It is about creating a system where teams continuously learn, adapt, and deliver valuable software.
