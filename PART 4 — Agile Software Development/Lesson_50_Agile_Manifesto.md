# Introduction

By the late 1990s many software teams faced the same problems:

- Projects took years.
- Requirements changed constantly.
- Documentation became more important than working software.
- Customers saw results too late.

In 2001, seventeen experienced software practitioners met to discuss a better way of building software.

The outcome became known as the **Agile Manifesto**.

---

# What is the Agile Manifesto?

The **Agile Manifesto** is a short statement of values that guides Agile software development.

It does **not** define a process.

Instead, it defines a mindset for making engineering decisions.

---

# Why Was It Created?

Traditional development often emphasized:

```text
Heavy Planning
      ↓
Long Development
      ↓
Late Feedback
      ↓
High Cost of Change
```

Agile proposed:

```text
Small Delivery
      ↓
Early Feedback
      ↓
Continuous Improvement
```

---

# The Four Values

## 1. Individuals and Interactions
### over Processes and Tools

People solve problems.

Processes and tools help people, but they should never replace communication.

### Industry Example

Instead of waiting three days for a document approval, a developer and tester discuss the issue for five minutes and solve it immediately.

---

## 2. Working Software
### over Comprehensive Documentation

Documentation is useful.

Working software creates business value.

The goal is to keep documentation valuable rather than excessive.

### Example

```text
20 Pages of Documents
        OR
Working Login Feature

Business prefers the working feature.
```

---

## 3. Customer Collaboration
### over Contract Negotiation

Customers should participate throughout development.

Regular feedback reduces misunderstandings.

```text
Customer
    ↕
Agile Team
    ↕
Continuous Feedback
```

---

## 4. Responding to Change
### over Following a Plan

Plans are important.

However, software projects change.

Agile teams adapt instead of resisting change.

### Example

A payment provider changes its API.

Instead of waiting for the next annual release, the team updates the software during the next sprint.

---

# Important Note

The manifesto does **not** reject the items on the right.

It says the items on the left are valued **more**.

```text
Processes ✔
Tools ✔
Documentation ✔
Contracts ✔
Plans ✔

People, software, collaboration,
and adaptability come first.
```

# Agile Manifesto in Industry

Imagine an online banking application.

Without Agile:

- Requirements fixed for one year.
- Customer feedback arrives after release.

With Agile:

```text
Sprint 1
   ↓
Customer Review
   ↓
Sprint 2
   ↓
Customer Review
   ↓
Sprint 3
```

The product evolves with business needs.

# Common Misconceptions

❌ Agile means no documentation.

✔ Agile encourages useful documentation.

❌ Agile means no planning.

✔ Agile plans continuously.

❌ Agile means customers decide everything.

✔ The development team still applies engineering judgment.

# Summary

The Agile Manifesto defines the values that shape modern software development. Rather than eliminating planning, documentation, or processes, it prioritizes people, working software, customer collaboration, and adaptability. These values enable teams to deliver useful software more quickly while responding effectively to changing business needs.
