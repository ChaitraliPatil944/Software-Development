# Introduction

The Agile Manifesto defines **4 core values**.

The **Twelve Agile Principles** explain how those values are applied in real software projects.

```text
Agile Manifesto
      |
4 Values
      |
12 Principles
      |
Daily Software Development
```

---

# Why Do We Need the Principles?

Values describe **what is important**.

Principles explain **how to act on those values**.

Example:

```text
Value:
Respond to Change

↓

Principle:
Welcome changing requirements,
even late in development.
```

---

# The 12 Agile Principles

## Principle 1 — Customer Satisfaction

**Highest priority:** satisfy the customer through **early and continuous delivery** of valuable software.

### Industry Example

Instead of delivering after one year:

```text
Sprint 1 → Login
Sprint 2 → Payments
Sprint 3 → Reports
```

Customers receive value continuously.

---

## Principle 2 — Welcome Change

Accept changing requirements, even late in development.

Business environments change rapidly.

Good Agile teams adapt.

---

## Principle 3 — Deliver Frequently

Deliver working software every few weeks rather than every few months.

Benefits:

- Faster feedback
- Lower risk
- Earlier value

---

## Principle 4 — Business & Developers Work Together

Business experts and developers collaborate throughout the project.

```text
Business
    ↕
Product Owner
    ↕
Developers
```

Better communication reduces misunderstandings.

---

## Principle 5 — Build Around Motivated Individuals

Provide:

- Trust
- Support
- Resources

Motivated teams produce better software.

---

## Principle 6 — Face-to-Face Communication

Direct conversation is usually the most effective way to exchange information.

Today this includes:

- Office discussions
- Video meetings
- Pair programming

---

## Principle 7 — Working Software is Progress

Progress is measured by **working software**, not by the number of documents or meetings.

```text
Prototype Working ✔
100-page report ✖
```

---

## Principle 8 — Sustainable Development

Maintain a pace that can continue over time.

Avoid constant overtime and burnout.

---

## Principle 9 — Technical Excellence

High-quality engineering enables agility.

Examples:

- Clean code
- Refactoring
- Automated testing
- Code reviews

---

## Principle 10 — Simplicity

Maximize the amount of work **not** done.

Build only what delivers value.

Avoid unnecessary features.

---

## Principle 11 — Self-Organizing Teams

Teams decide **how** work should be completed.

Managers define goals.

Teams choose implementation.

---

## Principle 12 — Continuous Improvement

Teams regularly reflect and improve.

Typical cycle:

```text
Sprint
   ↓
Retrospective
   ↓
Improve Process
   ↓
Next Sprint
```

# Common Misconceptions

❌ Agile means no discipline.

✔ Agile requires disciplined engineering.

❌ Agile ignores architecture.

✔ Good architecture supports agility.

❌ Agile encourages endless changes.

✔ Changes are prioritized based on business value.

# Summary

The Twelve Agile Principles transform the Agile Manifesto into practical guidance for software teams. They emphasize customer value, collaboration, adaptability, technical excellence, sustainable development, and continuous improvement. Together, these principles enable Agile teams to deliver high-quality software while responding effectively to changing business needs.
