# Introduction

A Sprint produces a Product Increment.

But building software is not enough.

The team must verify:

- Is the product moving in the right direction?
- Does it provide customer value?
- What should be improved next?

This happens during the **Sprint Review**.

```text
Sprint Work
      ↓
Product Increment
      ↓
Sprint Review
      ↓
Feedback
      ↓
Backlog Updates
```

---

# What is Sprint Review?

Sprint Review is a Scrum event held at the end of a Sprint where the Scrum Team presents the completed Product Increment to stakeholders and collects feedback.

---

# Purpose of Sprint Review

Sprint Review helps teams:

- Inspect the product outcome
- Gather stakeholder feedback
- Discuss future improvements
- Adapt the Product Backlog

---

# When Does Sprint Review Happen?

Sprint Review happens:

```text
End of Every Sprint
```

Example:

2-week Sprint:

```text
Week 1
Development

Week 2
Development + Testing

End
Sprint Review
```

## 1. Scrum Team

- Developers

- Demonstrate completed work

- Discuss improvements

## 2. Stakeholders

Examples:

- Customers
- Business leaders
- Users
- Management

Responsibilities:

- Provide feedback
- Share business needs
- Suggest improvements

# What Happens During Sprint Review?

## 1. Product Increment Demonstration

The team shows working software.

Example:

Food Delivery App:

Completed:

✓ Restaurant Search  
✓ Order Placement  
✓ Payment Feature

---

## 2. Feedback Collection

Stakeholders provide feedback.

Example:

Customer:

"Add delivery tracking."

Product Owner:

Adds requirement to Product Backlog.

---

## 3. Product Backlog Adjustment

Based on feedback:

- New items added
- Priorities changed
- Existing items updated

---

# Sprint Review vs Sprint Demo

| Sprint Review | Sprint Demo |
|---|---|
| Official Scrum event | Informal demonstration |
| Includes feedback and discussion | Mainly showing features |
| Updates product direction | Shows progress |

A demo can be part of a Sprint Review, but Sprint Review is broader.

---

# Sprint Review vs Sprint Retrospective

| Sprint Review | Sprint Retrospective |
|---|---|
| Focuses on product | Focuses on process |
| Includes stakeholders | Scrum Team only |
| Reviews increment | Improves teamwork |

---

# Benefits

- Customer alignment
- Faster feedback
- Better prioritization
- Increased transparency
- Reduced product risk

---

# Common Mistakes

## Mistake 1

Showing incomplete work.

Solution:

Demonstrate only completed increments.

---

## Mistake 2

Treating it as a presentation only.

Solution:

Encourage discussion and feedback.

---

## Mistake 3

Ignoring stakeholder input.

Solution:

Update backlog based on valuable feedback.

# Summary

Sprint Review allows Scrum teams and stakeholders to inspect the completed product increment and collaborate on future improvements. It is not just a feature demonstration; it is a feedback-driven event that helps ensure the product continues to deliver business and customer value.
