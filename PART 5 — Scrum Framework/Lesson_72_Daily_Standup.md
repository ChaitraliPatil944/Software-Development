# Introduction

A Sprint may last two weeks, but teams need daily coordination.

The Daily Standup helps the Scrum Team answer:

- Are we moving toward the Sprint Goal?
- Are there any blockers?
- Do we need to adapt our plan?

```text
Sprint Goal
     |
     ↓
Daily Communication
     |
     ↓
Progress Visibility
     |
     ↓
Better Delivery
```

---

# What is Daily Standup?

The **Daily Standup** (officially called **Daily Scrum**) is a short daily event where Developers inspect progress toward the Sprint Goal and adjust their plan.

---

# Purpose of Daily Standup

The Daily Standup exists to:

- Synchronize team members
- Identify blockers early
- Coordinate work
- Adapt the daily plan

It is not a meeting for reporting to managers.

---

# Duration

The Daily Scrum is usually:

```text
15 Minutes
```

It happens every working day during the Sprint.

---

# Participants

The main participants are:

## Developers

Responsible for:

- Sharing progress
- Discussing blockers
- Planning the day's work

---

## Scrum Master

Helps ensure:

- The meeting happens
- Scrum practices are understood

The Scrum Master does not control the discussion.

---

## Product Owner

May attend if useful.

They help clarify requirements but are not there to collect status reports.

---

# Three Daily Standup Questions

Traditionally teams discuss:

## 1. What did I complete yesterday?

Example:

"Completed payment API integration."

---

## 2. What will I work on today?

Example:

"Today I will add payment validation."

---

## 3. Are there any blockers?

Example:

"I need access to the testing environment."

---

# Why Daily Standup Matters

Without Daily Scrum:

```text
Small Problem
      ↓
Ignored
      ↓
Becomes Big Problem
      ↓
Sprint Failure
```

With Daily Scrum:

```text
Problem Found Early
      ↓
Team Solves It
      ↓
Sprint Continues
```

---

# Daily Standup vs Status Meeting

| Daily Standup | Status Meeting |
|---|---|
| Team synchronization | Reporting progress |
| Focus on Sprint Goal | Focus on management updates |
| Developers communicate | Manager receives updates |
| Problem solving after meeting | Usually discussion based |

---

# Common Mistakes

## Mistake 1

Turning it into a manager report.

Wrong:

"What percentage of work is completed?"

Better:

"What do we need to do to achieve the Sprint Goal?"

---

## Mistake 2

Making the meeting too long.

Solution:

Keep it focused.

---

## Mistake 3

Solving every problem during the meeting.

Solution:

Identify blockers and discuss details separately.

---

## Mistake 4

Skipping the meeting when things are busy.

Solution:

Busy periods require more coordination.

# Summary

The Daily Standup is a short synchronization event that helps Scrum teams inspect progress, identify blockers, and adjust their daily plans. It improves transparency and communication while keeping everyone focused on the Sprint Goal. A good Daily Standup is not about reporting activity; it is about enabling collaboration and solving problems quickly.
