# Introduction

Scrum is widely used in software companies because modern products constantly change.

Industry teams use Scrum to:

- Deliver features faster
- Receive continuous feedback
- Improve collaboration
- Manage complex projects

```text
Business Need
      ↓
Product Team
      ↓
Scrum Process
      ↓
Working Software
      ↓
Customer Feedback
      ↓
Improvement
```

# Scrum Challenges in Industry

## 1. Resistance to Change

Problem:

Teams used to traditional methods may resist Agile.

Solution:

Training and gradual adoption.

---

## 2. Poor Product Ownership

Problem:

Unclear priorities create confusion.

Solution:

Strong Product Owner involvement.

---

## 3. Too Many Meetings

Problem:

Teams perform ceremonies without purpose.

Solution:

Focus on Agile values, not only meetings.

---

## 4. Distributed Teams

Problem:

Communication becomes difficult.

Solution:

Use collaboration tools and clear processes.

# Scrum vs Traditional Enterprise Development

|---|---|

| Customer feedback | Feedback later |

| Cross-functional teams | Separate departments |

# Summary

Scrum is widely adopted in the software industry because it provides a practical way to manage complex products. Companies use Scrum with engineering practices like Git, CI/CD, automated testing, and cloud deployment to deliver reliable software faster. Successful Scrum depends on collaboration, transparency, strong ownership, and continuous improvement.
