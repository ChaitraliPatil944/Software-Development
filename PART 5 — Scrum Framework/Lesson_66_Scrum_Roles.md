# Part 5 — Scrum Framework

> **Objective**
>
> Understand the three official Scrum roles, their responsibilities, authority, collaboration patterns, and how each role contributes to successful product delivery.

---

# Introduction

Scrum does not use traditional project hierarchy.

Instead, Scrum defines three primary roles:

```text
            Scrum Team

        Product Owner
              |
        Scrum Master
              |
          Developers
```

Together, they work toward a common goal:

**Deliver valuable product increments.**

---

# Scrum Team

A Scrum Team is:

- Cross-functional
- Self-managing
- Focused on product goals
- Responsible for delivering increments

The team consists of:

1. Product Owner
2. Scrum Master
3. Developers

---

# Role 1 — Product Owner

## Purpose

The Product Owner maximizes the value of the product.

The Product Owner represents:

- Customers
- Users
- Business stakeholders

---

# Product Owner Responsibilities

## 1. Define Product Vision

The Product Owner answers:

- What problem are we solving?
- Who are our users?
- What value should we deliver?

Example:

"Build a food delivery platform that allows customers to order meals easily."

---

## 2. Manage Product Backlog

Responsibilities:

- Create backlog items
- Prioritize features
- Refine requirements
- Remove outdated items

---

## 3. Prioritize Business Value

Example:

Instead of:

```
Feature A
Feature B
Feature C
```

Product Owner decides:

```
High Priority
Payment System

Medium Priority
User Profile

Low Priority
Theme Selection
```

---

## 4. Accept Completed Work

The Product Owner verifies whether features meet expectations.

---

# Product Owner Skills

Important skills:

- Business understanding
- Communication
- Decision making
- Customer understanding
- Prioritization

---

# Role 2 — Scrum Master

## Purpose

The Scrum Master helps the team use Scrum effectively.

The Scrum Master is:

- Coach
- Facilitator
- Servant leader

Not a traditional manager.

---

# Scrum Master Responsibilities

## 1. Facilitate Scrum Events

Helps organize:

- Sprint Planning
- Daily Scrum
- Sprint Review
- Sprint Retrospective

---

## 2. Remove Impediments

Examples:

- Access problems
- Communication issues
- Process blockers

Example:

Developers cannot deploy because infrastructure access is missing.

Scrum Master coordinates resolution.

---

## 3. Coach the Team

Helps teams understand:

- Scrum principles
- Agile mindset
- Continuous improvement

---

## 4. Protect the Team

Prevents unnecessary interruptions during Sprint execution.

---

# Scrum Master Skills

- Leadership
- Communication
- Conflict resolution
- Coaching
- Problem solving

---

# Role 3 — Developers

## Purpose

Developers create the product increment.

Developers include:

- Software Engineers
- QA Engineers
- Designers
- Technical specialists

---

# Developer Responsibilities

## 1. Build Features

Activities:

- Design
- Coding
- Testing
- Integration

---

## 2. Maintain Quality

Developers ensure:

- Clean code
- Testing
- Reviews
- Maintainability

---

## 3. Estimate Work

Developers participate in:

- Story estimation
- Sprint planning
- Technical discussions

---

## 4. Self-Manage Work

Developers decide:

- How tasks are completed
- Technical approach
- Implementation strategy

---

# Scrum Roles Collaboration

```text
                Customer

                   |
                   ↓

            Product Owner
                   |
        Product Requirements

                   ↓

            Scrum Team

      Scrum Master ↔ Developers

                   ↓

          Product Increment
```

---

# Product Owner vs Scrum Master

| Product Owner | Scrum Master |
|---|---|
| Focuses on product value | Focuses on process |
| Manages backlog | Facilitates Scrum |
| Represents customer | Supports team |
| Decides priorities | Removes blockers |

---

# Scrum Master vs Project Manager

| Scrum Master | Project Manager |
|---|---|
| Servant leader | Manager |
| Coaches team | Controls execution |
| Removes blockers | Manages resources |
| Agile focus | Traditional project focus |

---

# Developers vs Product Owner

| Developers | Product Owner |
|---|---|
| Build the product | Defines product needs |
| Technical decisions | Business decisions |
| Estimate effort | Prioritize value |

---

# Industry Example

## Banking Application

Product Owner:

- Prioritizes mobile banking features

Scrum Master:

- Removes process blockers

Developers:

- Build APIs, UI, and tests

QA:

- Ensures quality

Together:

Deliver secure banking updates every Sprint.

# Common Mistakes

❌ Product Owner assigning tasks to developers.

✔ Developers decide implementation.

❌ Scrum Master managing people.

✔ Scrum Master enables the team.

❌ Developers ignoring business needs.

✔ Teams collaborate with Product Owner.

# Summary

Scrum roles create a balance between business value, team effectiveness, and technical execution. The Product Owner focuses on maximizing product value, the Scrum Master enables the Scrum process, and Developers build high-quality increments. Together, they form a self-managing team capable of continuously delivering valuable software.
