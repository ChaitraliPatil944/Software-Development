# Introduction

Successful teams do not only deliver software.

They continuously improve the way they work.

The Sprint Retrospective provides a dedicated time for the Scrum Team to inspect their process and identify improvements.

```text
Previous Sprint
       ↓
Reflection
       ↓
Identify Problems
       ↓
Create Improvements
       ↓
Better Next Sprint
```

---

# What is Sprint Retrospective?

Sprint Retrospective is a Scrum event where the Scrum Team discusses:

- What went well?
- What problems occurred?
- How can the team improve?

It happens after Sprint Review and before the next Sprint begins.

---

# Purpose of Sprint Retrospective

The Retrospective helps teams:

- Improve collaboration
- Improve processes
- Remove repeated problems
- Increase productivity
- Improve quality

---

# Participants

The Retrospective includes the entire Scrum Team:

## Product Owner

Provides business perspective.

---

## Scrum Master

Facilitates discussion.

---

## Developers

Share technical challenges and improvement ideas.

---

# Retrospective Workflow

```text
Sprint Completed

      ↓

Collect Feedback

      ↓

Discuss Problems

      ↓

Find Root Causes

      ↓

Create Action Items

      ↓

Apply Improvements
```

---

# Three Important Questions

## 1. What Went Well?

Examples:

- Good communication
- Successful deployment
- Better testing process

---

## 2. What Did Not Go Well?

Examples:

- Delayed testing
- Unclear requirements
- Deployment issues

---

## 3. What Should We Improve?

Examples:

- Add automation tests
- Improve documentation
- Refine backlog earlier

---

# Common Retrospective Techniques

# 1. Start, Stop, Continue

One of the most popular formats.

```text
START
Things we should begin doing

STOP
Things we should avoid

CONTINUE
Things working well
```

Example:

Start:

Automated testing

Stop:

Late requirement changes

Continue:

Code reviews

---

# 2. What Went Well / What Went Wrong

Simple reflection method.

```text
Positive
   |
Problems
   |
Solutions
```

---

# 3. 4Ls Technique

## Liked

What did we enjoy?

## Learned

What did we discover?

## Lacked

What was missing?

## Longed For

What improvements do we want?

---

# Action Items

A good Retrospective should create measurable actions.

Bad:

"Improve testing."

Good:

"Increase automated test coverage by 20% in next Sprint."

---

# Industry Example

## E-Commerce Application

Problem:

Checkout testing delays releases.

Retrospective Discussion:

Cause:

Manual testing takes too long.

Action:

Create automated checkout tests.

Next Sprint:

Testing becomes faster.

---

# Sprint Retrospective vs Sprint Review

| Sprint Retrospective | Sprint Review |
|---|---|
| Focuses on process | Focuses on product |
| Scrum Team only | Includes stakeholders |
| Improves teamwork | Collects product feedback |
| Internal improvement | External feedback |

---

# Benefits

- Continuous improvement
- Better teamwork
- Faster delivery
- Improved quality
- Reduced repeated problems
- Stronger communication

---

# Common Mistakes

## Mistake 1

Blaming individuals.

Solution:

Focus on process improvement.

---

## Mistake 2

Discussing problems without action.

Solution:

Create clear improvement tasks.

---

## Mistake 3

Skipping retrospectives.

Solution:

Every Sprint should include learning.

---

# Industry Example

## Banking Application Team

Issue:

Production bugs increased.

Retrospective:

Root Cause:

Insufficient automated testing.

Improvement:

Add regression test automation.

Result:

Reduced production defects.

# Summary

Sprint Retrospective is the engine of continuous improvement in Scrum. It allows teams to reflect on their process, identify problems, and create actionable improvements. A successful retrospective creates learning, not blame, helping teams become more effective with every Sprint.
