# Introduction

A Scrum team needs someone who understands:

- Customer needs
- Business goals
- Market requirements
- Product direction

This responsibility belongs to the **Product Owner**.

The Product Owner connects business expectations with the development team.

```text
Customer Needs
       |
       ↓
 Product Owner
       |
       ↓
Development Team
       |
       ↓
Working Product
```

---

# What is a Product Owner?

A **Product Owner (PO)** is the Scrum role responsible for maximizing the value of the product created by the Scrum Team.

The Product Owner decides:

- What should be built?
- What provides maximum value?
- What should be prioritized?

---

# Why Does the Product Owner Exist?

Without a Product Owner:

```text
Many Stakeholders
       |
Different Opinions
       |
No Clear Priority
       |
Development Confusion
```

The Product Owner creates alignment.

---

# Core Responsibilities

## 1. Product Vision

The Product Owner defines the long-term direction.

Questions:

- What problem are we solving?
- Who are the users?
- What value are we creating?

Example:

Product Vision:

"Create a mobile banking platform that allows customers to manage finances securely."

---

# 2. Product Backlog Ownership

The Product Owner manages the Product Backlog.

Responsibilities:

- Create backlog items
- Prioritize items
- Remove unnecessary work
- Refine requirements

Example:

```text
Product Backlog

1. Login
2. Payments
3. Reports
4. Notifications
5. Profile
```

---

# 3. Prioritization

Not every feature has equal importance.

The Product Owner considers:

- Business value
- Customer impact
- Risk
- Dependencies

---

# Prioritization Techniques

## MoSCoW Method

```text
M - Must Have
S - Should Have
C - Could Have
W - Won't Have
```

Example:

Food Delivery App:

Must Have:

- Login
- Order Placement

Could Have:

- Theme Customization

---

## Value vs Effort Matrix

```text
High Value + Low Effort
        |
        ↓
     Build First


Low Value + High Effort
        |
        ↓
     Delay
```

---

# 4. Creating User Stories

Product Owners define user needs.

Format:

```text
As a <user>
I want <feature>
So that <benefit>
```

Example:

"As a customer, I want to track my order so that I know delivery status."

---

# 5. Acceptance Criteria

Defines when a feature is acceptable.

Example:

Login Feature:

✓ User can enter credentials  
✓ Valid credentials allow access  
✓ Invalid credentials show error

---

# 6. Stakeholder Management

The Product Owner communicates with:

- Customers
- Business teams
- Management
- Development team

The PO balances different expectations.

---

# Product Owner Daily Activities

Typical activities:

Morning:

- Review backlog
- Clarify requirements

During Sprint:

- Answer developer questions
- Review progress

Sprint Review:

- Collect feedback
- Plan future priorities

---

# Product Owner Does NOT Do

❌ Assign tasks to developers

Developers decide how work is completed.

❌ Manage the technical solution

Developers decide implementation.

❌ Act as a project manager

PO focuses on product value.

---

# Product Owner vs Project Manager

| Product Owner | Project Manager |
|---|---|
| Product value | Project execution |
| Customer needs | Schedule and resources |
| Backlog priority | Project tracking |
| What to build | How to manage project |

---

# Product Owner vs Scrum Master

| Product Owner | Scrum Master |
|---|---|
| Product direction | Process improvement |
| Backlog ownership | Scrum facilitation |
| Business value | Team effectiveness |

# Industry Example

Sprint 1:

- Product search

Sprint 2:

- Cart improvements

Sprint 3:

- Payment optimization

# Common Mistakes

❌ Treating backlog as a task list.

✔ Backlog represents product value.

❌ Prioritizing based only on stakeholder pressure.

✔ Use business impact.

❌ Writing unclear requirements.

✔ Create clear user stories.

# Summary

The Product Owner is responsible for maximizing product value by connecting customers, business goals, and development teams. Through product vision, backlog management, prioritization, and stakeholder collaboration, the Product Owner ensures that the Scrum team builds the most valuable features first.
