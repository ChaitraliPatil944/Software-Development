# What is Scrum?

**Scrum** is an Agile framework used to develop, deliver, and maintain complex products through iterative and incremental development.

Scrum organizes work into short development cycles called **Sprints**.

---

# Core Idea of Scrum

```text
Product Goal
      ↓
Product Backlog
      ↓
Sprint Planning
      ↓
Sprint
      ↓
Increment
      ↓
Feedback
      ↓
Next Sprint
```

---

# Why Was Scrum Created?

Traditional development faced problems:

- Long release cycles
- Poor communication
- Late feedback
- Difficulty handling change

Scrum introduced:

- Short iterations
- Frequent inspection
- Continuous improvement
- Team collaboration

---

# Scrum Philosophy

Scrum is based on:

## Empiricism

Making decisions based on:

- Observation
- Experience
- Evidence

```text
Plan
 ↓

 ↓

 ↓

```

---

# Three Pillars of Scrum

## 1. Transparency

Everyone understands:

- Goals
- Progress
- Problems

Example:

Sprint board visible to the team.

---

## 2. Inspection

Teams regularly inspect:

- Product progress
- Sprint progress
- Process effectiveness

Examples:

- Daily Scrum
- Sprint Review

---

## 3. Adaptation

Teams adjust when problems are discovered.

Example:

If testing is slow, the team improves the process.

---

# Scrum Values

Scrum has five core values:

## 1. Commitment

Team members commit to goals.

## 2. Focus

Concentrate on important sprint work.

## 3. Openness

Share progress and problems honestly.

## 4. Respect

Trust team members and their expertise.

## 5. Courage

Address difficult problems.

# Summary

Scrum is one of the most popular Agile frameworks used in software development. It provides a structured way for teams to apply Agile principles through defined roles, events, and artifacts. By working in short Sprints, inspecting progress frequently, and adapting continuously, Scrum teams deliver valuable software in changing environments.
