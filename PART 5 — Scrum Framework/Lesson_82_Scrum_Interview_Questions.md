# Lesson 82 — Scrum Interview Questions

# Part 5 — Scrum Framework

> **Objective**
>
> Prepare for Scrum interviews by understanding frequently asked questions, role-based questions, scenario-based discussions, and practical Scrum concepts used in software engineering interviews.

---

# Section 1 — Scrum Fundamentals

## Q1. What is Scrum?

**Answer:**

Scrum is an Agile framework used to develop complex products through iterative development, short Sprints, continuous feedback, and incremental delivery.

---

## Q2. How is Scrum related to Agile?

Scrum is one implementation framework of Agile.

```text
Agile
 |
 ├── Scrum
 ├── Kanban
 └── XP
```

Agile defines values and principles.

Scrum defines roles, events, and artifacts.

---

## Q3. What are the three pillars of Scrum?

The three pillars are:

1. Transparency
2. Inspection
3. Adaptation

---

## Q4. What are Scrum values?

Five Scrum values:

- Commitment
- Focus
- Openness
- Respect
- Courage

---

# Section 2 — Scrum Roles

## Q5. What are the Scrum roles?

Scrum has three main roles:

1. Product Owner
2. Scrum Master
3. Developers

---

## Q6. What is the role of Product Owner?

The Product Owner:

- Defines product vision
- Manages Product Backlog
- Prioritizes features
- Maximizes product value

---

## Q7. Who owns the Product Backlog?

The Product Owner owns the Product Backlog.

However, the entire Scrum Team contributes to refinement.

---

## Q8. Can Product Owner assign tasks to Developers?

No.

Developers decide how work is completed.

---

## Q9. What is the role of Scrum Master?

The Scrum Master:

- Facilitates Scrum events
- Removes blockers
- Coaches the team
- Improves Scrum adoption

---

## Q10. Is Scrum Master a Project Manager?

No.

A Scrum Master is a servant leader, not a traditional manager.

---

# Section 3 — Sprint Questions

## Q11. What is a Sprint?

A Sprint is a fixed time-box where a Scrum Team creates a usable product increment.

---

## Q12. How long is a Sprint?

A Sprint is usually:

```text
1 to 4 weeks
```

Most companies use:

```text
2-week Sprints
```

---

## Q13. What happens during a Sprint?

Activities include:

- Development
- Testing
- Integration
- Daily Scrum
- Progress tracking

---

## Q14. What is Sprint Goal?

Sprint Goal is the objective that explains why the Sprint is valuable.

Example:

"Enable customers to complete online payments."

---

# Section 4 — Scrum Events

## Q15. What are Scrum events?

Scrum events include:

1. Sprint
2. Sprint Planning
3. Daily Scrum
4. Sprint Review
5. Sprint Retrospective

---

## Q16. What happens during Sprint Planning?

The team decides:

- Sprint Goal
- Selected backlog items
- Implementation approach

---

## Q17. What happens during Daily Scrum?

Developers discuss:

- Progress
- Next steps
- Blockers

---

## Q18. Difference between Sprint Review and Retrospective?

| Sprint Review | Sprint Retrospective |
|---|---|
| Product focused | Process focused |
| Stakeholders participate | Scrum Team participates |
| Collect feedback | Improve teamwork |

---

# Section 5 — Scrum Artifacts

## Q19. What are Scrum artifacts?

Three Scrum artifacts:

1. Product Backlog
2. Sprint Backlog
3. Product Increment

---

## Q20. What is Product Backlog?

A prioritized list of everything needed for the product.

---

## Q21. What is Sprint Backlog?

A collection of work selected for the current Sprint.

Owned by Developers.

---

## Q22. What is Product Increment?

A usable and potentially releasable version of the product.

---

# Section 6 — Estimation Questions

## Q23. What are Story Points?

Story Points measure relative effort, complexity, risk, and uncertainty.

---

## Q24. Why does Scrum use Story Points?

Because exact time estimation is difficult.

Story Points provide relative comparison.

---

## Q25. Why is Fibonacci used?

Because uncertainty increases as work becomes larger.

Example:

```
1 2 3 5 8 13 21
```

---

## Q26. What is Planning Poker?

A collaborative estimation technique where team members estimate stories and discuss differences.

---

# Section 7 — Metrics Questions

## Q27. What is Velocity?

Velocity is the amount of Story Points completed by a team in a Sprint.

---

## Q28. Can Velocity measure developer performance?

No.

Velocity measures team capacity, not individual performance.

---

## Q29. What is a Burndown Chart?

A chart showing remaining work during a Sprint.

---

# Section 8 — Scenario-Based Questions

## Scenario 1

A Product Owner adds new requirements during a Sprint.

What should happen?

Answer:

Discuss impact with the team and protect the Sprint Goal.

---

## Scenario 2

The team cannot complete Sprint work.

What should they do?

Answer:

Analyze:

- Estimation accuracy
- Blockers
- Capacity
- Process issues

---

## Scenario 3

A developer is blocked by another team.

What happens?

Answer:

Scrum Master helps remove the impediment.

---

## Scenario 4

Stakeholders are unhappy with product direction.

What should happen?

Answer:

Use Sprint Review feedback and update Product Backlog priorities.

---

# Section 9 — Comparison Questions

## Scrum vs Waterfall

| Scrum | Waterfall |
|---|---|
| Iterative | Sequential |
| Flexible | Fixed |
| Continuous feedback | Feedback at end |
| Frequent releases | Single release |

---

## Scrum Master vs Project Manager

| Scrum Master | Project Manager |
|---|---|
| Facilitates | Manages |
| Coaches | Controls |
| Removes blockers | Tracks resources |

# Interview Tips

Good Scrum answers should include:

✓ Definition  
✓ Purpose  
✓ Real example  
✓ Business impact  

Avoid only memorizing definitions.

---

# Summary

Scrum interviews test practical understanding of Agile execution. A strong candidate should explain Scrum roles, events, artifacts, estimation, metrics, and real-world situations. Understanding why each Scrum practice exists is more valuable than memorizing terminology.
