# Lesson 65 — Scrum Workflow

# Part 5 — Scrum Framework

> **Objective**
>
> Understand the complete Scrum workflow, how work moves from an idea to a delivered product increment, and how Scrum teams operate during every Sprint.

---

# Introduction

Scrum creates a repeating workflow where teams:

- Plan work
- Build features
- Inspect progress
- Collect feedback
- Improve continuously

```text
Plan → Build → Review → Improve → Repeat
```

---

# Step 1 — Product Vision

Every Scrum project starts with a goal.

The Product Vision explains:

- What problem are we solving?
- Who are the users?
- What value will we provide?

Example:

"Create a platform where customers can order food easily."

---

# Step 2 — Product Backlog

The Product Backlog contains all possible work required for the product.

Example:

```text
Product Backlog

1. User Registration
2. Restaurant Search
3. Order Placement
4. Payment
5. Delivery Tracking
```

The Product Owner manages priorities.

---

# Step 3 — Sprint Planning

The Scrum Team decides what work will be completed in the upcoming Sprint.

Questions:

1. What is the Sprint Goal?
2. Which backlog items should be selected?
3. How will the team complete them?

Output:

```text
Sprint Goal
      +
Sprint Backlog
```

---

# Step 4 — Sprint Execution

A Sprint is a fixed time period where the team builds the product increment.

During the Sprint:

Developers:

- Design
- Code
- Test
- Integrate

QA:

- Validate features
- Report defects

Product Owner:

- Clarifies requirements

Scrum Master:

- Removes blockers

---

# Step 5 — Daily Scrum

A short daily meeting.

Purpose:

Synchronize the team.

Questions:

```text
What did I complete?
        |
What will I do next?
        |
What is blocking me?
```

---

# Step 6 — Product Increment

At the end of development, the team creates a working product increment.

Example:

Sprint Goal:

"Implement user authentication"

Increment:

✓ Registration  
✓ Login  
✓ Password validation

---

# Step 7 — Sprint Review

The team demonstrates completed work to stakeholders.

Flow:

```text
Working Software
       |
       ↓
Demo
       |
       ↓
Feedback
       |
       ↓
Backlog Updates
```

Purpose:

Ensure the product is moving in the correct direction.

---

# Step 8 — Sprint Retrospective

The team improves its process.

Discussion:

- What went well?
- What problems occurred?
- What should change?

Example:

Problem:

Testing delays releases.

Improvement:

Increase automation testing.

# Scrum Workflow Benefits

- Clear direction
- Faster delivery
- Early feedback
- Reduced risk
- Continuous improvement
- Better transparency

---

# Common Mistakes

❌ Starting development without a Sprint Goal.

✔ Define clear Sprint objectives.

❌ Adding unlimited work during Sprint.

✔ Protect Sprint scope.

❌ Skipping Retrospectives.

✔ Improve after every Sprint.

# Summary

The Scrum workflow is a continuous cycle that transforms product ideas into working software through short Sprints. By combining planning, development, inspection, feedback, and improvement, Scrum enables teams to deliver valuable software while adapting to changing requirements.
