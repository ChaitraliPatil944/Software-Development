# Introduction

Sprint Planning is the event where the Scrum Team decides:

- What work will be completed?
- Why is this work important?
- How will the work be done?

It starts every Sprint.

```text
Product Backlog
        |
        ↓
 Sprint Planning
        |
        ↓
 Sprint Goal
        |
        ↓
 Sprint Backlog
```

---

# What is Sprint Planning?

Sprint Planning is a Scrum event where the team selects Product Backlog Items and creates a plan to deliver a valuable product increment during the Sprint.

---

# Why Do We Need Sprint Planning?

Without planning:

```text
Random Tasks
      ↓
Unclear Goal
      ↓
Poor Coordination
      ↓
Failed Sprint
```

With Sprint Planning:

```text
Clear Goal
      ↓
Selected Work
      ↓
Team Alignment
      ↓
Successful Sprint
```

---

# Participants

Sprint Planning involves the entire Scrum Team.

## 1. Product Owner

Responsibilities:

- Explain priorities
- Describe business value
- Clarify requirements

---

## 2. Scrum Master

Responsibilities:

- Facilitate the meeting
- Ensure Scrum rules are followed
- Help remove confusion

---

## 3. Developers

Responsibilities:

- Estimate effort
- Decide implementation approach
- Determine capacity

---

# Sprint Planning Inputs

Before planning begins:

## 1. Product Backlog

Contains prioritized work.

Example:

```text
High Priority

✓ User Login
✓ Payment
✓ Search

Low Priority

✓ Themes
```

---

## 2. Product Goal

Defines the overall direction.

Example:

"Create an online shopping platform."

---

## 3. Team Capacity

The team considers:

- Available developers
- Holidays
- Current workload
- Technical constraints

---

# Sprint Planning Process

```text
Step 1
Understand Product Goal

        ↓

Step 2
Review Product Backlog

        ↓

Step 3
Select Backlog Items

        ↓

Step 4
Create Sprint Goal

        ↓

Step 5
Break Work Into Tasks

        ↓

Step 6
Create Sprint Backlog
```

---

# Step 1 — Define Sprint Goal

The Sprint Goal explains the purpose of the Sprint.

Bad Goal:

"Complete five tasks."

Good Goal:

"Enable customers to complete online payments."

A goal provides direction.

---

# Step 2 — Select Product Backlog Items

The team selects items based on:

- Priority
- Complexity
- Capacity
- Dependencies

Example:

Sprint Capacity:

30 Story Points

Selected Work:

```text
Login          5 SP
Search         8 SP
Profile        5 SP

Total          18 SP
```

---

# Step 3 — Break Stories Into Tasks

Large stories are divided.

Example:

User Login Story:

```text
User Login

Tasks:

✓ Create Database Table
✓ Build API
✓ Create UI
✓ Add Validation
✓ Write Tests
```

---

# Step 4 — Create Sprint Backlog

The Sprint Backlog contains:

- Sprint Goal
- Selected stories
- Technical tasks

Example:

```text
Sprint Goal:
Implement Authentication

Sprint Backlog:

Login API
Login UI
Validation
Testing
```

---

# Sprint Planning Questions

The team discusses:

## What?

What features should be built?

## Why?

Why are these features valuable?

## How?

How will the team implement them?

# Sprint Planning Duration

Typical duration:

For a 2-week Sprint:

```text
Around 2–4 hours
```

Longer Sprints require longer planning.

---

# Common Mistakes

## Mistake 1

Selecting too much work.

Solution:

Use historical velocity.

---

## Mistake 2

No Sprint Goal.

Solution:

Define a clear outcome.

---

## Mistake 3

Only developers decide priorities.

Solution:

Product Owner provides business priorities.

---

## Mistake 4

Only focusing on coding.

Solution:

Include testing and quality activities.

---

# Sprint Planning vs Daily Scrum

| Sprint Planning | Daily Scrum |
|---|---|
| Before Sprint | Every day |
| Creates plan | Tracks progress |
| Longer meeting | Short meeting |
| Defines Sprint Goal | Identifies blockers |

# Summary

Sprint Planning creates alignment before development begins. The Product Owner explains business priorities, Developers decide implementation and capacity, and the Scrum Master facilitates the process. A successful Sprint Planning session creates a clear Sprint Goal, realistic Sprint Backlog, and shared understanding among the entire Scrum Team.
