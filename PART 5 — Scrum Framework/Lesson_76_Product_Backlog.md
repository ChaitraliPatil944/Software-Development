# Introduction

Every software product starts with ideas, problems, and requirements.

The Product Backlog organizes all possible work needed to build the product.

```text
Product Vision
      ↓
Product Backlog
      ↓
Sprint Planning
      ↓
Sprint Backlog
      ↓
Product Increment
```

---

# What is Product Backlog?

The **Product Backlog** is an ordered list of everything that may be needed in a product.

It contains:

- Features
- Enhancements
- Bug fixes
- Technical improvements
- Research tasks

The Product Backlog evolves continuously as the product grows.

---

# Why Do We Need Product Backlog?

Without a Product Backlog:

```text
Many Ideas
    ↓
No Priority
    ↓
Confusion
    ↓
Poor Product Decisions
```

With a Product Backlog:

```text
Ideas
 ↓
Prioritization
 ↓
Development Plan
 ↓
Customer Value
```

---

# Product Backlog Ownership

The Product Backlog is owned by:

```text
Product Owner
```

The Product Owner is responsible for:

- Ordering backlog items
- Maximizing product value
- Ensuring clarity

---

# Product Backlog Structure

A Product Backlog may contain:

```text
Epic
  ↓
Feature
  ↓
User Story
  ↓
Tasks
```

Example:

```text
Epic:
Online Payments

Feature:
Payment Gateway Integration

User Story:
As a customer,
I want to pay online,
so that I can complete my order.
```

---

# Product Backlog Items

Common backlog items:

## 1. Features

New product capabilities.

Example:

"Add dark mode."

---

## 2. User Stories

User-focused requirements.

Example:

"As a user, I want password reset so that I can recover my account."

---

## 3. Bugs

Issues that need fixing.

Example:

"Payment fails for international cards."

---

## 4. Technical Improvements

Engineering work.

Example:

"Upgrade database version."

---

# Product Backlog Characteristics

A good Product Backlog is:

## Ordered

Highest-value items come first.

---

## Dynamic

Changes as business needs change.

---

## Transparent

Visible to the Scrum Team.

---

## Refined

Continuously improved.

---

# Product Backlog Refinement

Backlog refinement is the activity of preparing future backlog items.

During refinement:

- Requirements are clarified
- Large items are split
- Estimates are added
- Priorities are reviewed

```text
Large Feature
      ↓
Smaller User Stories
      ↓
Ready for Sprint Planning
```

---

# Prioritization Techniques

## 1. MoSCoW Method

```text
M - Must Have
S - Should Have
C - Could Have
W - Won't Have
```

Example:

Food Delivery App:

Must Have:

- Login
- Order Placement

Could Have:

- Theme Customization

---

# 2. Business Value Prioritization

Features are ranked by:

- Customer impact
- Revenue impact
- Strategic importance

---

# 3. Value vs Effort Matrix

```text
High Value + Low Effort

        ↓

      Build First


Low Value + High Effort

        ↓

       Delay
```

# Product Backlog vs Sprint Backlog

| Product Backlog | Sprint Backlog |
|---|---|
| Entire product work | Current Sprint work |
| Owned by Product Owner | Owned by Developers |
| Long-term view | Short-term execution |
| Continuously refined | Updated during Sprint |

---

# Product Backlog vs Requirement Document

| Product Backlog | Requirement Document |
|---|---|
| Living artifact | Usually fixed document |
| Continuously updated | Created during analysis |
| Prioritized items | Detailed requirements |

# Common Mistakes

## Mistake 1

Treating backlog as a simple task list.

Solution:

Backlog represents product value.

---

## Mistake 2

Everything marked as high priority.

Solution:

Prioritize based on business impact.

---

## Mistake 3

Ignoring backlog refinement.

Solution:

Keep future work ready.

# Summary

The Product Backlog is the foundation of Scrum planning. It contains all potential work required to build and improve a product. Owned by the Product Owner, it is continuously refined and prioritized to ensure that Scrum teams always work on the highest-value items. A healthy Product Backlog creates clarity, alignment, and efficient product development.
